# Odysseus and the Sirens

*A retelling of the classic Greek myth from Homer's Odyssey, for young readers*

---

## The Story

Long, long ago there was a brave sailor named Odysseus. He had been away from home for a very long time, fighting in a big war far across the sea. Now he just wanted to get back to his family, his wife Penelope, and his son Telemachus.

One day, the goddess Circe warned Odysseus about a terrible danger he would soon face ‚Äî a group of creatures called the **Sirens**.

The Sirens sat on some rocks in the middle of the ocean. They were beautiful, and they could sing the most magical, beautiful songs anyone had ever heard. But there was a very dark secret: their song was a *trap*. Every sailor who heard the Sirens' song was so enchanted that he forgot everything ‚Äî his family, his ship, his home ‚Äî and sailed straight toward the rocks. The ships would crash and the sailors would never be seen again.

Odysseus wanted very much to hear the famous song. But he also knew it was dangerous. So he came up with a clever plan.

He told his crew:

> "Put beeswax in your ears so you cannot hear the Sirens at all. Sail straight ahead and do not stop, no matter what happens."

Then he said something unusual:

> "But tie *me* tightly to the mast of the ship. I want to hear the song ‚Äî but do not untie me, no matter how much I beg or shout. Keep me tied until we are safely past."

His crew did exactly as he asked. When the ship drew close to the Sirens' island, Odysseus heard the song.

It was the most beautiful thing he had ever heard. He pulled and pulled at his ropes. He shouted at his crew to untie him. He begged and pleaded.

But his loyal crew kept rowing, looking straight ahead, ears full of wax, hearing nothing. They stayed the course.

And soon‚Ä¶ the song grew fainter. And then it disappeared.

They had made it safely past. Odysseus slumped against the mast, exhausted but alive.

When they were far enough away, the crew untied him, and Odysseus felt grateful. Without the ropes and the brave trust of his crew, he would have sailed to his doom.

## What This Story Is Teaching Us

### 1. üß† Know Your Own Weaknesses
Odysseus was very brave and very smart ‚Äî but he *knew* that even brave, smart people can be tricked. He didn't pretend he was too strong to be affected by the Sirens. Instead, he made a plan that accounted for his weakness. It's okay to say, "I know this might be hard for me, so I'm going to prepare."

### 2. üìã Plan Ahead
When you know a tricky moment is coming, you can make rules for yourself *before* you get there, when your thinking is clear. Odysseus gave his crew instructions long before the ship reached the Sirens. He didn't wait until the song started. By then it was too late. Good planning keeps us safe.

### 3. üõë It's Okay to Say No to Some Temptations, Even Beautiful Ones
The Sirens' song sounded wonderful. But it would have led Odysseus to his death. Not everything that looks or sounds amazing is good for you. Some things are beautiful traps. Learning to notice the difference is one of the most important things you'll ever do.

### 4. ü§ù Trust the People Who Love You
Odysseus trusted his crew to keep him safe, even when *he himself* was begging them to stop. Real friendship sometimes means doing what is best for someone, not what they're asking for in a panicked moment. And real courage sometimes means trusting others to help you when you can't help yourself.

### 5. üè† Keep Your Eyes on What Really Matters
The Sirens promised wonderful things ‚Äî knowledge, pleasure, adventure. But Odysseus had something even better waiting for him: his family back home. When you remember what truly matters most to you, it's easier to stay on the right path, even when distractions show up.

## A Few Words to Think About

| Word | What it means |
|------|---------------|
| **Temptation** | When something makes you *really* want to do a thing, even if it isn't good for you |
| **Enchanted** | Put under a magic spell; so charmed that you can't think clearly |
| **Self-control** | The ability to stop yourself from doing something, even when part of you really wants to |
| **Loyalty** | Staying true and helpful to someone you care about, even when it's hard |
